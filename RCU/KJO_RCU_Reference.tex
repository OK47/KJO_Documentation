\section{Operator Interface (GUI)}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Button Layout}

Six buttons are arranged in a 2$\times$3 grid on the left side of the screen:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
Button & Row & Col & Command Sent \\
\midrule
Fill   & 1 & 1 & \cmd{BEGIN\_FILL} \\
Dump   & 1 & 2 & \cmd{DUMP} (latching toggle; second press $\to$ \cmd{STOP\_DUMP}) \\
Reset  & 2 & 1 & \cmd{RESET} \\
Status & 2 & 2 & \cmd{RCU\_STATUS} \\
Enable & 3 & 1 & \cmd{ENABLE\_LCO\_CONTROL} \\
Launch & 3 & 2 & \cmd{START\_ENGINE} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

All buttons highlight on press and auto-clear after 1~second.
The \textbf{Dump} button is an exception: it remains highlighted until a second
press is detected (latching behaviour), confirming the dump is active.

\subsection{Valve Status Indicators}

Four valve symbols occupy the right portion of the screen:
Fill, N\textsubscript{2}O (Ox), Fuel, and Dump.
Each symbol uses a circle-and-bar motif: a vertical bar on a green disc
indicates open; a horizontal bar on a red disc indicates closed.
State is updated from the \cmd{HEARTBEAT} response packet.

\subsection{Status Panels}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Battery voltages}: RCU voltage (measured locally on A7);
        EMU voltage (received in heartbeat).
  \item \textbf{Link status}: LINK\_OK / LINK\_MARGINAL / LINK\_DOWN, with
        current RSSI value.
  \item \textbf{Time and temperature}: Time from DS3231; temperature
        converted to \textdegree F.
  \item \textbf{Message log}: Scrolling text panel showing last TX/RX events.
\end{itemize}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Radio Command Dispatch}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\sw{RCU\_Execute\_Command(command\_ID, p1)} builds and transmits a command
packet.  \cmd{HEARTBEAT} uses the \texttt{Heartbeat\_Command\_Packet} structure
(6~bytes, includes RCU voltage); all other commands use \texttt{Command\_Payload}
(4~bytes, includes a \texttt{short} parameter).

Responses are parsed in \sw{Check\_and\_Process\_Receive\_Packet()}.
The \cmd{HEARTBEAT} response is routed to \sw{Process\_Heartbeat\_Response()},
which updates EMU voltage and all four valve symbols.

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{SD Logging}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Log files are stored in \texttt{RCU\_Logs/Log\_N.txt} (N incremented to avoid
overwriting existing logs).  Each entry is time-stamped from the DS3231 and
records all TX and RX events in human-readable form, e.g.:
\begin{lstlisting}[language={},numbers=none]
10:15:03 [TX] BEGIN_FILL (1) | param: 0
10:15:03 [RX] BEGIN_FILL (1) | status: OK | r_val: 0 | RSSI: -72
\end{lstlisting}
