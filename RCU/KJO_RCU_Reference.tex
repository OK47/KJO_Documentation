\section{Operator Interface (GUI)}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Button Layout and Behaviour}

Six buttons are arranged in a 2$\times$3 grid on the left side of the screen.
Each button sends one or more commands depending on its mode:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lllp{7cm}@{}}
\toprule
Button & Row & Col & Behaviour \\
\midrule
Fill    & 1 & 1 & \textbf{Latching toggle.} First press $\to$ \cmd{BEGIN\_FILL} (button stays highlighted).
                  Second press $\to$ \cmd{STOP\_FILL} (button clears). \\
Dump    & 1 & 2 & \textbf{Latching toggle.} First press $\to$ \cmd{DUMP} (button stays highlighted).
                  Second press $\to$ \cmd{STOP\_DUMP} (button clears). \\
Reset   & 2 & 1 & \textbf{Immediate ABORT.} Sends \cmd{RESET}; clears all latched button states
                  (Fill, Dump, Enable). \\
Status  & 2 & 2 & Sends \cmd{RCU\_STATUS}; auto-clears after 1~s. \\
Enable  & 3 & 1 & \textbf{Hold-to-enable LCO.} Press sends \cmd{ENABLE\_LCO\_CONTROL} (param~=~1,
                  button highlighted); finger-lift sends \cmd{ENABLE\_LCO\_CONTROL} (param~=~0,
                  button clears). \\
Launch  & 3 & 2 & \textbf{Guarded by confirmation dialog.} Press opens a modal CONFIRM / CANCEL
                  overlay.  CONFIRM sends \cmd{START\_ENGINE}; CANCEL dismisses without sending.
                  See §\ref{sec:confirm_dialog}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Valve Status Indicators}

Four valve symbols occupy the right portion of the screen:
Fill, N\textsubscript{2}O (Ox), Fuel, and Dump.
Each symbol uses a circle-and-bar motif: a vertical bar on a green disc
indicates open; a horizontal bar on a red disc indicates closed.
State is updated from the \cmd{HEARTBEAT} response packet.

When the tank pressure exceeds \texttt{PRESSURE\_IN\_SYSTEM\_THRESHOLD}
(as reported in heartbeat responses), the background of the valve panel
turns \textbf{red} as a visual pressure warning (see §\ref{sec:pressure_warning}).

\subsection{Status Panels}

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Battery voltages}: RCU voltage (measured locally on A7);
        EMU voltage (received in heartbeat).
  \item \textbf{Link status}: LINK\_OK / LINK\_MARGINAL / LINK\_DOWN, with
        current RSSI value.
  \item \textbf{Time and temperature}: Time from DS3231; temperature
        converted to \textdegree F.
  \item \textbf{Message log}: Scrolling text panel showing last TX/RX events.
\end{itemize}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Engine Start Confirmation Dialog}
\label{sec:confirm_dialog}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Pressing the \textbf{Launch} button does \emph{not} immediately send
\cmd{START\_ENGINE}.  Instead, a modal overlay is drawn over the main screen
with two large buttons:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{CONFIRM} (green) --- sends \cmd{START\_ENGINE} to the EMU
        and dismisses the overlay.
  \item \textbf{CANCEL} (red) --- dismisses the overlay without transmitting
        any command.
\end{itemize}

The overlay cannot be dismissed by touching outside of it; only CONFIRM or
CANCEL can close it.  This prevents an accidental single touch from initiating
the ignition sequence.

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Pressure Warning}
\label{sec:pressure_warning}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\sw{Check\_Pressure\_Status()} is called every loop iteration.  It compares
the most recent tank pressure value (carried in the heartbeat response packet's
\texttt{r\_val} field, or from the last \cmd{READ\_TANK\_PRESSURE} response)
against \texttt{PRESSURE\_IN\_SYSTEM\_THRESHOLD}.

When pressure exceeds the threshold:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item The valve symbol panel background is redrawn in \textbf{red}.
  \item A ``PRESSURISED'' warning label is displayed in the status area.
\end{itemize}

When pressure returns below the threshold (or a \cmd{RESET} response is
received), the panel background returns to its normal colour and the warning
label is cleared.

This visual warning mirrors the EMU-side audible pressure alarm (continuous
buzzer on the EMU) so the operator at the RCU can immediately see that the
propellant system is under pressure.

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Radio Command Dispatch}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\sw{RCU\_Execute\_Command(command\_ID, p1)} builds and transmits a command
packet.  \cmd{HEARTBEAT} uses the \texttt{Heartbeat\_Command\_Packet} structure
(6~bytes, includes RCU voltage); all other commands use \texttt{Command\_Payload}
(4~bytes, includes a \texttt{short} parameter).

Responses are parsed in \sw{Check\_and\_Process\_Receive\_Packet()}.
The \cmd{HEARTBEAT} response is routed to \sw{Process\_Heartbeat\_Response()},
which updates EMU voltage and all four valve symbols.

\subsection{Command Reference}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
Command & Sent by & Notes \\
\midrule
\cmd{HEARTBEAT}               & Automatic (every 5~s) & RCU voltage in payload; EMU returns all valve positions \\
\cmd{RESET}                   & Reset button          & ABORT; clears all latched button states on RCU \\
\cmd{BEGIN\_FILL}             & Fill button (1st press) & Starts propellant fill; EMU starts pressure recording \\
\cmd{STOP\_FILL}              & Fill button (2nd press) & Stops propellant fill; recording continues until ambient \\
\cmd{DUMP}                    & Dump button (1st press) & Opens Dump valve \\
\cmd{STOP\_DUMP}              & Dump button (2nd press) & Closes Dump valve \\
\cmd{ENABLE\_LCO\_CONTROL}    & Enable button (hold / release) & param 1 = enable LCO remote start; param 0 = disable \\
\cmd{START\_ENGINE}           & Launch + CONFIRM        & Arms 7-step ignition sequence on EMU \\
\cmd{RCU\_STATUS}             & Status button           & Requests EMU status summary \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{SD Logging}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Log files are stored in \texttt{RCU\_Logs/Log\_N.txt} (N incremented to avoid
overwriting existing logs).  Each entry is time-stamped from the DS3231 and
records all TX and RX events in human-readable form, e.g.:
\begin{lstlisting}[language={},numbers=none]
10:15:03 [TX] BEGIN_FILL (1) | param: 0
10:15:03 [RX] BEGIN_FILL (1) | status: OK | r_val: 0 | RSSI: -72
\end{lstlisting}
