\subsection{Class Hierarchy}

\texttt{Valve} (base) $\longleftarrow$ \texttt{Slave\_Valve} (derived).

The \sw{Valve} base class provides servo drive and potentiometer feedback.
\sw{Slave\_Valve} adds edge-detection polling of \sig{AUX\_IO\_1} (CMD) and
output driving of \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} (STATUS).

\texttt{QR\_Servo} (base) $\longleftarrow$ \texttt{QR\_Slave} (derived).

\sw{QR\_Servo} provides servo drive via the PCA9685 with time-based motion
completion detection (no potentiometer).  \sw{QR\_Slave} adds level-based
CMD following (\sig{AUX\_IO\_3}: LOW = open latch, HIGH = hold latch),
physical separation detection via \sig{AUX\_IO\_4} (\sig{RELEASE\_STATE\_EIO}),
and a local Button~A override (\sw{localRelease()} / \sw{localHold()}).

\section{Fill Valve Control (\sw{Slave\_Valve})}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Slave\_Valve Operation}

\sw{Slave\_Valve.update()} is called every loop iteration.  It performs
edge detection on the CMD line (\sig{AUX\_IO\_1}) and responds as follows:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
CMD transition & Slave\_Valve action & STATUS (\sig{AUX\_IO\_2}) \\
\midrule
LOW $\to$ HIGH (rising edge) & Drive servo to fully open  & Asserted HIGH when open \\
HIGH $\to$ LOW (falling edge)& Drive servo to fully closed & Asserted HIGH when closed \\
No change                   & Poll servo position         & Unchanged \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

STATUS is asserted as soon as the potentiometer confirms the valve has
reached the commanded position (within dead-band).

\subsection{Local Button Override}

Three panel buttons on the MCP23X17 provide local control during ground
operations:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Button & Action \\
\midrule
A (QR Release) & \textbf{Hold-to-release}: calls \sw{localRelease()} on press; servo opens
                 while button held.  Calls \sw{localHold()} on release; servo returns to
                 hold position.  Event logged to SD card. \\
B (Fill Close) & Drive Fill valve servo to fully closed position (falling-edge triggered). \\
C (Fill Open)  & Drive Fill valve servo to fully open position (falling-edge triggered). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{warningbox}
Button~A activates the umbilical quick-release servo.  The latch opens for
as long as the button is held, and releases pressurised
N\textsubscript{2}O when the umbilical separates.  Ensure all personnel are
clear and the pad is safe before pressing Button~A.
Buttons~B and~C bypass EMU command authority; use for ground testing only.
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Handshake from GSEMU Perspective}

The sequence from the GSEMU point of view:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item Poll \sig{AUX\_IO\_1}: if rising edge detected, enter OPENING state.
  \item Drive servo toward fully open; \sw{Valve::update()} monitors pot.
  \item When \sw{isOpen()} returns true, assert \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} = HIGH.
  \item Poll \sig{AUX\_IO\_1}: if falling edge detected, enter CLOSING state.
  \item Drive servo to closed; assert \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} = HIGH when complete.
  \item De-assert \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} after a brief confirmation hold.
\end{enumerate}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Umbilical Quick-Release (\sw{QR\_Slave})}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Overview}

The umbilical quick-release servo physically separates the umbilical connector
from the rocket airframe at launch.  The GSEMU drives the servo; the EMU
commands the release via a level-based GPIO protocol on two AUX connector
lines.  The \sw{QR\_Slave} class manages the servo, the CMD-level follower,
and physical separation detection.

\subsection{Hardware}

The QR servo is driven by a channel on the PCA9685 Servo Wing.  Unlike the
Fill valve servo, there is no potentiometer feedback; position is determined
entirely by elapsed time (\sw{QR\_SERVO\_MOVE\_MS}).

Two MCP23X17 expansion I/O lines are used:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \sig{AUX\_IO\_3} (\sig{QR\_CMD\_EIO}) --- INPUT\_PULLUP.
        Reads the CMD level from the EMU.
  \item \sig{AUX\_IO\_4} (\sig{RELEASE\_STATE\_EIO}) --- wired to GSEMU chassis
        GND.  When read by the EMU as INPUT\_PULLUP: LOW = umbilical connected,
        HIGH = connector removed (separated).
\end{itemize}

\begin{warningbox}
\texttt{QR\_SERVO\_PWM\_HOLD} and \texttt{QR\_SERVO\_PWM\_OPEN} are
placeholder values in the current firmware.  They \textbf{must} be calibrated
on the bench before any live operation.  Verify servo travel direction and
endpoint positions before connecting the umbilical.
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Signal Protocol}

\sig{AUX\_IO\_3} (\sig{QR\_CMD\_EIO}) is driven by the EMU (\sw{QR\_Master}).
The GSEMU reads it with INPUT\_PULLUP on every \sw{update()} call:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\sig{QR\_CMD\_EIO} Level & Meaning & Servo action \\
\midrule
HIGH (default) & Latch hold / armed & Servo commanded to HOLD position \\
LOW            & Release commanded  & Servo commanded to OPEN position \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

When the AUX connector is disconnected, INPUT\_PULLUP floats HIGH --- the
servo defaults to HOLD (safe, latch engaged).

\subsection{Level-Follower Operation}

\sw{QR\_Slave::update()} is called every \sw{loop()} iteration.  It reads
the current level of \sig{QR\_CMD\_EIO} (or uses the local override flag if
Button~A is held) and drives the servo accordingly:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item CMD LOW and servo not already at or moving to OPEN $\Rightarrow$
        call \sw{openServo()}.
  \item CMD HIGH and servo not already at or moving to HOLD $\Rightarrow$
        call \sw{holdServo()}.
\end{itemize}

The servo holds its last commanded position while the CMD level is
unchanged.  There is no terminal/latch state: the servo will return to HOLD
any time the CMD line goes HIGH (e.g., EMU calls \sw{hold()}, power cycle,
or AUX cable disconnection).

\subsection{Local Button A Override}

Button~A on the GSEMU front panel engages a local override that bypasses the
CMD line.  \sw{Check\_Buttons()} calls:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \sw{QR\_Release.localRelease()} on button press (falling edge).
  \item \sw{QR\_Release.localHold()} on button release (rising edge).
\end{itemize}

While the override is active (\sw{\_local\_override = true}), \sw{update()}
substitutes LOW for the CMD pin read --- the servo stays open regardless of
what the EMU is driving.  The override is cleared on button release; the servo
returns to CMD-line following on the next \sw{loop()} iteration.

\subsection{QR\_Slave API}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Method & Action \\
\midrule
\sw{begin()}           & Configure CMD and state pins; command servo to HOLD \\
\sw{update()}          & Poll CMD (or override), drive servo; detect separation \\
\sw{isSeparated()}     & Returns true (latched) once state line has gone HIGH \\
\sw{isConnected()}     & Returns true while state line reads LOW \\
\sw{localRelease()}    & Set local override; servo opens on next \sw{update()} \\
\sw{localHold()}       & Clear local override; servo follows CMD on next \sw{update()} \\
\sw{isLocalOverride()} & Returns true while Button~A override is active \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{OLED Display}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The SH1107 OLED provides local status when no serial console is available.
\sw{KJO\_GSE\_Display} provides:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \sw{scrollMessage(msg)}: scrolls a text message across the display.
  \item \sw{displayValveStatus(pct)}: shows the current valve open percentage.
  \item \sw{displayLogEntry(msg)}: echoes SD log entries to the display.
\end{itemize}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{SD Logging}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Log files are stored in the root of the SD card as \texttt{Log\_N.txt}
(N is auto-incremented to avoid overwriting existing files).
Each record is time-stamped (PCF8523) and logs CMD/STATUS transitions, servo
position changes, button presses, and system events.
