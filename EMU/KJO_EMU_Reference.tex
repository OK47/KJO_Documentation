\section{Valve Control Subsystem}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Valve Base Class (\sw{KJO\_Valve})}

The \sw{Valve} class encapsulates a servo-driven, potentiometer-feedback valve:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Drive}: PWM signal to PCA9685 servo wing channel.
  \item \textbf{Feedback}: Potentiometer on ADS1015 ADC channel; position
        expressed as 0--100\% open.
  \item \textbf{Position control}: \sw{openTo(\%)} sets target; \sw{update()}
        drives servo until pot reading reaches target within dead-band.
  \item \textbf{State queries}: \sw{isMoving()}, \sw{isStopped()},
        \sw{isOpen()}, \sw{isClosed()}.
\end{itemize}

The EMU operates as a top-level state machine with three states:

\subsection{Engine Start Sequence (7 Steps)}

When \cmd{START\_ENGINE} or a LCO panel button press is received, the EMU
enters \texttt{ENGINE\_STARTING} and advances through seven steps
non-blockingly in \sw{Check\_Engine\_Start()}:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}clp{9cm}@{}}
\toprule
Step & Trigger & Action \\
\midrule
0 & immediate   & Verify Ox, Fuel, Dump valves are closed; abort on failure \\
1 & step 0 pass & Command umbilical QR release; start 3~s separation timer \\
2 & timer       & Poll \sw{isSeparated()}; abort on timeout \\
3 & separation  & Fire igniter relay (\texttt{IGNITER\_FIRE\_DURATION\_MS} pulse);
                  start delay-1 timer \\
4 & delay-1     & Open Ox feed valve \\
5 & Ox stopped  & Start delay-2 timer \\
6 & delay-2     & Open Fuel feed valve \\
7 & Fuel stopped& Send OK response; set \sw{engine\_running = true} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Any abort at steps 0--2 sends a \texttt{Command\_Return\_Packet} with
\texttt{status = false} and an \texttt{r\_val} error code
($-1$ = precondition failure, $-2$ = QR timeout).
\cmd{RESET} cancels the sequence at any step.

\subsection{Servo Mechanical Geometry and Calibration}

\subsubsection*{Gear Train and Servo Travel}

Each valve is actuated by a ServoCity 2000-0025-0504 5-turn servo through a
16-tooth pinion driving a 60-tooth valve gear:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Parameter & Value \\
\midrule
Gear ratio                             & $60 \div 16 = \mathbf{3.75:1}$ \\
Valve travel (open $\to$ close)        & 90\textdegree \\
Required servo rotation                & $90 \times 3.75 = 337.5$\textdegree \\
Servo full mechanical range            & 1800\textdegree\ (5 turns) over 500--2500~$\mu$s \\
PCA9685 frequency                      & 1100~Hz (non-standard; improves count resolution) \\
PWM period at 1100~Hz                  & $1 \div 1100 = 909.09~\mu$s \\
$\mu$s per 12-bit count                & $909.09 \div 4096 \approx 0.2219~\mu$s/count \\
$\mu$s for 337.5\textdegree\ servo travel& $337.5 \times (2000~\mu\text{s} \div 1800\textdegree) = 375~\mu$s \\
\textbf{Theoretical $\Delta$ counts}   & $375 \div 0.2219 \approx \mathbf{1690}$ counts \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{notebox}
Running the PCA9685 at 1100~Hz (instead of the standard 50~Hz) compresses the
usable pulse-width span into the lower portion of the 12-bit count range,
effectively improving resolution over the $\sim$1.1 turns of servo travel
actually used.
\end{notebox}

\subsubsection*{Empirical Calibration (March 2026)}

Bench testing showed the theoretical 1690-count span caused all four valves to
travel noticeably \emph{more} than 90\textdegree.  All four valves were
re-calibrated.  The empirical open-to-close deltas average $\approx$1583~counts
($\sim$6\% below theoretical), consistent with the servo being slightly more
sensitive in the operating band than the linear model predicts.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrrrrrrrr@{}}
\toprule
Valve & \multicolumn{4}{c}{Servo PWM (12-bit counts)} & \multicolumn{3}{c}{Pot ADC (12-bit counts)} \\
\cmidrule(lr){2-5}\cmidrule(lr){6-8}
 & Open & Close & $\Delta$ & Implied travel & Open & Closed & $\Delta$ \\
\midrule
Fill     & 2179 & 3719 & 1540 & $\sim$82.1\textdegree & 1238 & 2984 & 1746 \\
Oxidizer & 2050 & 3690 & 1640 & $\sim$87.4\textdegree & 1139 & 2857 & 1718 \\
Fuel     & 2130 & 3780 & 1650 & $\sim$87.9\textdegree & 1170 & 2918 & 1748 \\
Dump     & 2229 & 3729 & 1500 & $\sim$79.9\textdegree & 1329 & 3055 & 1726 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Master\_Valve Class (\sw{KJO\_Master\_Valve})}

\sw{Master\_Valve} implements the CMD/STATUS GPIO handshake for
the Fill valve.  The Fill valve servo and pot reside on the GSEMU;
the EMU controls it via \sig{AUX\_IO\_1} (CMD) and monitors
\sig{AUX\_IO\_2} (STATUS).

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Fill Valve Handshake Protocol}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The Fill valve servo and potentiometer reside on the GSEMU.
The EMU and GSEMU exchange state using two GPIO lines:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
Signal & Driver & Meaning when HIGH \\
\midrule
\sig{AUX\_IO\_1} (CMD)    & EMU   & Fill valve commanded open \\
\sig{AUX\_IO\_2} (STATUS) & GSEMU & Fill valve has reached commanded position \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Open sequence:}
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item EMU calls \sw{Fill\_Valve.open()}.
  \item \sw{Master\_Valve} asserts CMD = HIGH.
  \item GSEMU \sw{Slave\_Valve.update()} detects CMD rising edge; drives servo to open.
  \item GSEMU asserts STATUS = HIGH when servo reaches open position.
  \item EMU reads STATUS = HIGH; reports completion.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Close sequence}: CMD de-asserted (LOW); GSEMU closes servo; STATUS driven LOW.

\section{Command Dispatch}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\sw{Check\_Radio()} receives packets from the RCU and routes them.
Commands are processed in priority order:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item \cmd{HEARTBEAT} — always processed; returns
        \texttt{Heartbeat\_Response\_Packet} with EMU voltage and
        all four valve positions.
  \item \cmd{RESET} — always processed regardless of state;
        calls \sw{Execute\_Abort()}.
  \item All other commands are \emph{dropped} if \texttt{emu\_state}
        is VALVE\_MOVING or ENGINE\_STARTING.
  \item Per-command interceptors (deferred valve moves, start sequence,
        recording).
  \item All remaining commands dispatched to \sw{Execute\_Command()}.
\end{enumerate}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Umbilical Quick-Release (\sw{QR\_Master})}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Overview}

\sw{QR\_Master} is the EMU-side proxy for the umbilical quick-release actuator
that resides on the GSEMU.  It drives two MCP23X17 expansion I/O lines:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \sig{AUX\_IO\_3} (\sig{QR\_CMD\_EIO}) --- OUTPUT.  Level-based command
        to the GSEMU QR servo.
  \item \sig{AUX\_IO\_4} (\sig{RELEASE\_STATE\_EIO}) --- INPUT\_PULLUP.  Wired
        to GSEMU chassis GND through the AUX connector.  Reads LOW while the
        umbilical is physically connected; floats HIGH when the connector is
        removed (separation confirmed).
\end{itemize}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
\sig{QR\_CMD\_EIO} Level & Driver & Meaning \\
\midrule
HIGH (default) & EMU & Latch engaged --- GSEMU servo held at hold position \\
LOW            & EMU & Latch open --- GSEMU servo commanded to release position \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{warningbox}
Ensure the launch sequence has been verified and all personnel are clear
before issuing \cmd{QR\_RELEASE}.  The umbilical disconnection releases
pressurised N\textsubscript{2}O.  Calling \sw{hold()} after \sw{release()}
returns the CMD line HIGH but cannot re-engage a latch that has already
opened mechanically.
\end{warningbox}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Buzzer and Visual Indicator}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Startup Self-Test}

At power-on, the buzzer fires \textbf{five short beeps} (75~ms on / 75~ms gap)
to confirm the buzzer and relay circuits are functional.  The NeoPixel cycles
red~$\to$ yellow~$\to$ green~$\to$ off as a pixel self-test.

\subsection{NeoPixel Indicator State Machine}

\sw{Check\_Indicator\_And\_Buzzer()} runs every loop iteration and updates the
32-pixel NeoPixel array to reflect the highest-priority active condition.
States are evaluated in descending priority:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lllp{4.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
Priority & Condition & Pattern & Colour \\
\midrule
1 & \sw{engine\_running} = true      & Off (all pixels dark) & --- \\
2 & \sw{emu\_state} = ENGINE\_STARTING & Solid                 & Red \\
3 & \sw{tank\_pressurized} = true    & Flash 300/500~ms       & Red \\
4 & \sw{link\_connected} = true      & Flash 300/1200~ms      & Green \\
5 & (unlinked)                        & Flash 300/1200~ms      & Yellow \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\texttt{link\_connected} is set true on every received \cmd{HEARTBEAT} and
cleared after 15~s of silence (loss-of-signal watchdog).
\texttt{tank\_pressurized} is updated by \sw{Check\_Pressure\_Recording()}
using the live tank pressure reading; the threshold is
$>$\,\texttt{PRESSURE\_WARNING\_THRESHOLD} (15~PSI).

\subsection{Buzzer Behaviour}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{8.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
Condition & Buzzer Action \\
\midrule
Startup self-test            & 5 beeps (75~ms on, 75~ms gap) --- blocking, in \sw{setup()} \\
Button press confirmation    & 1 or 2 short beeps (100~ms) via \sw{Beep()} \\
Pressure alarm               & \textbf{Continuous tone} while tank\_pressurized = true \\
Engine start / abort         & Alarm silenced immediately when \sw{Arm\_Engine\_Start()} or
                               \sw{Execute\_Abort()} is called \\
``I'm alive'' heartbeat      & Single 100~ms beep every 10~s; suppressed during alarm,
                               ENGINE\_STARTING, and ENGINE\_RUNNING \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The pressure alarm is managed entirely within \sw{Check\_Indicator\_And\_Buzzer()}.
When the indicator state transitions \emph{to} \texttt{IND\_PRESSURIZED},
\sw{Buzzer.on()} is called (no timeout — continuous).
When the state transitions \emph{away} from \texttt{IND\_PRESSURIZED},
\sw{Buzzer.off()} is called.
\sw{Arm\_Engine\_Start()} and \sw{Execute\_Abort()} both call
\sw{Buzzer.off()} directly and clear the \sw{pressure\_alarm\_active} flag
so the next \sw{Check\_Indicator\_And\_Buzzer()} call does not re-arm it.

\subsection{NeoPixel\_Indicator API}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Method & Action \\
\midrule
\sw{begin()}                             & Initialise hardware; clear all pixels \\
\sw{on(color)}                           & Set all pixels solid; cancel any flash or timed mode \\

\subsection{Pressure Transducer Signal Chain}

Each pressure transducer produces a ratiometric voltage output over
0.5--4.5~V corresponding to 0--full-scale PSI.  A voltage divider on the
A/D daughter board scales this to the ADS1015 input range:

\[
V_\text{ADC} = V_\text{transducer} \times \frac{3.3}{4.5}
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrrl@{}}
\toprule
Condition & Transducer (V) & After divider (V) & ADS1015 count (GAIN\_ONE) \\
\midrule
0 PSI (zero offset)  & 0.500 & 0.367 & $\approx$ \textbf{184} \\
Full-scale PSI       & 4.500 & 3.300 & $\approx$ \textbf{1657} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

ADS1015 at \texttt{GAIN\_ONE} ($\pm$4.096~V): $\approx$0.001991~V/count.
The two-point linear \sw{map()} in \sw{Pressure\_Sensor\_Average()} implicitly
performs all three conversion steps (undo divider, subtract 0.5~V offset,
scale 0--4~V to PSI) without explicit voltage arithmetic.

\subsection{Continuous Pressure Recording}

Continuous recording is started automatically by \cmd{BEGIN\_FILL} and may
also be started manually with \cmd{START\_PRESSURE\_RECORDING}.
Every \texttt{PRESSURE\_RECORDING\_INTERVAL\_MS} (250~ms) the EMU reads
tank pressure, chamber pressure, tank temperature, and chamber temperature
and appends all four values to the SD log.

Recording auto-stops when the tank pressure remains below
\texttt{PRESSURE\_AMBIENT\_THRESHOLD} (10~PSI) for four consecutive readings,
or when \cmd{STOP\_PRESSURE\_RECORDING} or \cmd{RESET} is received.

\section{SD Logging}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Log files are stored in the root of the SD card as \texttt{Log\_N.txt}
(N is auto-incremented to avoid overwriting existing files).
Each record is time-stamped (PCF8523) and records command receipt, valve
movements, sensor readings, and fault events.
