\subsection{Class Hierarchy}

\texttt{Valve} (base) $\longleftarrow$ \texttt{Slave\_Valve} (derived).

The \sw{Valve} base class provides servo drive and potentiometer feedback.
\sw{Slave\_Valve} adds edge-detection polling of \sig{AUX\_IO\_1} (CMD) and
output driving of \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} (STATUS).

\texttt{QR\_Servo} (base) $\longleftarrow$ \texttt{QR\_Slave} (derived).

\sw{QR\_Servo} provides servo drive via the PCA9685 with time-based motion
completion detection (no potentiometer).  \sw{QR\_Slave} adds the
\sig{AUX\_IO\_3} rising-edge state machine for the umbilical release sequence.

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Fill Valve Control (\sw{Slave\_Valve})}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Slave\_Valve Operation}

\sw{Slave\_Valve.update()} is called every loop iteration.  It performs
edge detection on the CMD line (\sig{AUX\_IO\_1}) and responds as follows:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
CMD transition & Slave\_Valve action & STATUS (\sig{AUX\_IO\_2}) \\
\midrule
LOW $\to$ HIGH (rising edge) & Drive servo to fully open  & Asserted HIGH when open \\
HIGH $\to$ LOW (falling edge)& Drive servo to fully closed & Asserted HIGH when closed \\
No change                   & Poll servo position         & Unchanged \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

STATUS is asserted as soon as the potentiometer confirms the valve has
reached the commanded position (within dead-band).

\subsection{Local Button Override}

Three panel buttons on the MCP23X17 allow the GSEMU to control the Fill
valve independently of the EMU, useful during ground testing:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
Button & Action \\
\midrule
A (Open)  & Drive Fill valve servo to fully open position \\
B (Close) & Drive Fill valve servo to fully closed position \\
C (Status)& Display current valve position (\%) on OLED \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{warningbox}
Local button overrides bypass the EMU command authority.  Use for
ground testing only.  During a fill or engine operation sequence, local
overrides should not be used.
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Handshake from GSEMU Perspective}

The sequence from the GSEMU point of view:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item Poll \sig{AUX\_IO\_1}: if rising edge detected, enter OPENING state.
  \item Drive servo toward fully open; \sw{Valve::update()} monitors pot.
  \item When \sw{isOpen()} returns true, assert \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} = HIGH.
  \item Poll \sig{AUX\_IO\_1}: if falling edge detected, enter CLOSING state.
  \item Drive servo to closed; assert \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} = HIGH when complete.
  \item De-assert \sig{AUX\_IO\_2} after a brief confirmation hold.
\end{enumerate}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Umbilical Quick-Release (\sw{QR\_Slave})}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Overview}

The umbilical quick-release servo physically separates the umbilical connector
from the rocket airframe at ignition.  The GSEMU drives the servo; the EMU
commands the release via a single GPIO line (\sig{AUX\_IO\_3}).  The
\sw{QR\_Slave} class manages the servo and the release state machine.

\subsection{Hardware}

The QR servo is driven by a channel on the PCA9685 Servo Wing.  Unlike the
Fill valve servo, there is no potentiometer feedback; position is determined
entirely by elapsed time (\sw{QR\_SERVO\_MOVE\_MS}).

\begin{warningbox}
\texttt{QR\_SERVO\_PWM\_HOLD} and \texttt{QR\_SERVO\_PWM\_OPEN} are
placeholder values in the current firmware.  They \textbf{must} be calibrated
on the bench before any live operation.  Verify servo travel direction and
endpoint positions before connecting the umbilical.
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Signal Protocol}

\sig{AUX\_IO\_3} is driven by the EMU (\sw{QR\_Master}).
The GSEMU reads it with INPUT\_PULLUP:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
\sig{AUX\_IO\_3} state & Meaning \\
\midrule
LOW (default / armed) & System armed; servo held at hold position \\
Rising edge (LOW $\to$ HIGH) & Release commanded by EMU \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The GSEMU does \textbf{not} send a STATUS reply for the QR release — the
operation is fire-and-forget.  The EMU is responsible for confirming
successful separation by other means (e.g., accelerometer, visual inspection).

\subsection{State Machine}

\sw{QR\_Slave.update()} is called every loop iteration and advances through
the following states:

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\begin{tikzpicture}[node distance=2.2cm]
  \node[statenode] (armed)    {ARMED};
  \node[statenode, right=of armed]   (opening) {OPENING};
  \node[statenode, right=of opening] (released){RELEASED};
  \node[statenode, below=1.6cm of opening] (closing) {CLOSING};
  \node[statenode, left=2.2cm of closing]  (done)    {DONE};
  \draw[statetransition] (armed)    edge node[above,font=\tiny]{rising edge} (opening);
  \draw[statetransition] (opening)  edge node[above,font=\tiny]{timer expired} (released);
  \draw[statetransition] (released) edge node[right,font=\tiny]{hold elapsed} (closing);
  \draw[statetransition] (closing)  edge node[above,font=\tiny]{timer expired} (done);
  \draw[statetransition, loop below, looseness=5] (done) edge (done);
\end{tikzpicture}
\caption{QR\_Slave release state machine.}
\end{figure}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
State & Servo position & Condition to advance \\
\midrule
ARMED    & HOLD   & Rising edge on \sig{AUX\_IO\_3} \\
OPENING  & Moving toward OPEN & \sw{QR\_SERVO\_MOVE\_MS} elapsed \\
RELEASED & OPEN   & \sw{QR\_RELEASE\_HOLD\_MS} elapsed \\
CLOSING  & Moving toward HOLD & \sw{QR\_SERVO\_MOVE\_MS} elapsed \\
DONE     & HOLD   & Terminal state; stays here until power-cycle \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{OLED Display}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The SH1107 OLED provides local status when no serial console is available.
\sw{KJO\_GSE\_Display} provides:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \sw{scrollMessage(msg)}: scrolls a text message across the display.
  \item \sw{displayValveStatus(pct)}: shows the current valve open percentage.
  \item \sw{displayLogEntry(msg)}: echoes SD log entries to the display.
\end{itemize}

%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{SD Logging}
%═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Log files are stored in \texttt{GSEMU\_Logs/Log\_N.txt}.  Each record is
time-stamped (PCF8523) and logs CMD/STATUS transitions, servo position
changes, button presses, and system events.
